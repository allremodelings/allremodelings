For Homeowners

Remodeling Tips & Guides

🛁
Bathroom Remodeling
How to Plan Your Bathroom Renovation
Start with a clear budget, choose your tile and fixtures before demo, and always hire a licensed contractor. Good waterproofing is non-negotiable.
🎨
Interior Painting
Choosing the Right Paint Finish
Flat for ceilings, eggshell for living areas, semi-gloss for bathrooms and trim. Premium paints cover better and last longer.
🪵
Tile & Flooring
Porcelain vs. Ceramic Tile
Porcelain is denser and more water-resistant — ideal for wet areas. Ceramic is lighter and easier to cut, great for backsplashes and walls.
🧱
Drywall & Texture
What Is a Level 5 Drywall Finish?
Level 5 is the highest quality finish — a thin skim coat over the entire surface for a perfectly smooth, paint-ready wall. Standard for high-end homes.
🏠
Exterior Painting
Florida Exterior Painting Guide
Florida's sun and humidity require paints rated for tropical climates. Prep with pressure washing, caulk all gaps. A good job should last 7–10 years.
💡
Project Planning
5 Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Contractor
1) Licensed & insured? 2) Written estimate? 3) Who does the work? 4) Timeline? 5) How do you handle surprises? Any pro answers all five clearly.
Get Prepared

Project Checklists

Use these checklists to prepare for your remodeling project and make the most of your estimate appointment.

Before Your Estimate Appointment
Know which rooms or areas you want to remodel
Have a rough budget range in mind
Gather inspiration photos (Pinterest, Houzz, etc.)
Note specific materials or finishes you prefer
List any concerns (water damage, old tile, etc.)
Know your preferred timeline or move-in date
Before Work Begins
Confirm contractor is licensed and insured
Get everything in writing — scope, cost, timeline
Clear the work area and protect furniture
Confirm tile, paint, and materials are ordered
Discuss daily work hours and access
Establish a communication method (call/text)
